Order Sync
Case study · small-business automation

Three order queues.
One list for the kitchen.

Two lightweight AI agents on top of an ordinary Google Sheet freed a café's kitchen from manually re-tallying orders — no separate POS system, no new interface for staff to learn.

01 · The problem

Orders arrive grouped by customer.
The kitchen needs the opposite.

Three sources feed orders in at once: a walk-in customer at the counter, a form on the website, a phone call. Each order looks like one person's list of dishes — "Ivan: salad, sandwich, croissant." The kitchen needs the reverse cut: how many salads, sandwiches, and croissants need cooking right now, and in what order. That recalculation used to be done by a person, by hand — extra dishes, delays, no real prioritization.

02 · The fix

Two agents, one spreadsheet

Agent 1 pulls orders from all three sources into one sheet, marks them new, and archives them by day and month. Agent 2 pulls only the new orders every 30 minutes, re-tallies them by dish, and updates the kitchen's working list — accounting for what's already been cooked and what hasn't.

03 · Every 30 minutes

The one field the kitchen touches by hand

The "Total status" column is the only manual input. Everything else syncs on its own, following three rules:

  1. 1done and new orders for that dish arrived → the quantity is recalculated from scratch, status resets to new.
  2. 2A number is set (the kitchen noted how much is still left to cook) → the new batch is added on top of that number.
  3. 3No new orders arrived → the row is left untouched, and if it was done, it sinks to the bottom of the list.
stateDiagram-v2
    [*] --> new: New dish in the orders
    new --> number: Kitchen starts cooking
    new --> done: Batch finished
    number --> number: No new orders
    number --> number: New orders: B = number + new sum
    done --> done: No new orders → sinks down
    done --> new: New orders → recalculated back to top

05 · Budget and hosting

Three ways to build it

From free to fully custom. For one café with a simple flow, the first is usually enough.

Google Apps Script
Runs inside Google — no hosting to manage.
$300–600one-time
$0–50/ month
  • ✓ Launch in 3–5 days
  • ✓ Zero infrastructure cost
  • ✓ Everything lives in Sheets
No-code (n8n / Make)
Cloud subscription, or self-hosted on your own server.
$400–800one-time
$50–150/ month
  • ✓ Launch in 5–7 days
  • ✓ Visual builder, easy to extend
  • ✓ Ready-made integrations (WhatsApp, CRM)
Custom service
Node/Python on a VPS or PaaS (Render, Railway).
$800–1500one-time
$105–225/ month
  • ✓ Launch in 10–14 days
  • ✓ Full control of the logic
  • ✓ Room for custom analytics later
